So… what actually is it?

It’s like having a brilliant, endlessly patient colleague sitting at your computer who can read your files and do real tasks for you. You just ask, in normal words.

  • An assistant that runs on your computer, or in a private online workspace you open, and can actually do things, not just chat. It writes documents, translates, fixes spreadsheets, drafts emails, and researches for you.
  • It can open and work with your real files: your registration list, your sponsor brochure, that 40-page PDF, the conference programme you’re building.
  • Made by Anthropic, an AI company. Yes, it can help with software too, but that’s not what matters for us. It’s here to help with the everyday Med&X work.

Imagine hiring the most capable assistant you have ever met. Fluent in English and formal Croatian, tireless, never annoyed, working in seconds. Now sit them down at your own computer. Because they can see your files, you can just say “take our English invitation and make a polished formal Croatian version,” or “this sponsor spreadsheet is a mess, clean it up and count our attendees by city,” and they simply do it. That is what this is: not a search box you copy and paste into, but a colleague with hands, working right where your real documents live.

Trust & safety

Is it safe? Yes, here’s how

A human always stays in charge and reviews the work. For a medical NGO, that matters, so here are the simple habits that keep everything private and correct.

  • Always read an AI-written email, letter, or message yourself before sending it. Treat every draft as a smart first draft from an assistant. You are the one who hits send, and your name is on it.
  • Never paste patient-identifiable information (names, dates of birth, OIB, medical record numbers, diagnoses tied to a real person) into any task. Med&X handles conference and sponsor work, not patient care, so keep it that way. This protects patient privacy and keeps you GDPR-compliant.
  • Double-check every fact before it goes out: speaker titles and affiliations, dates, venues, prices, deadlines, and sponsorship amounts. AI can occasionally state something confidently that is slightly off, so verify anything a reader will act on.
  • Verify the numbers in any document it produces: attendee counts, budget totals, headcounts by city or role. Ask it to show how it got the number, or spot-check a few rows against the original file yourself.
  • For anything official (invitations, brochures, certificates, translations sent to sponsors or institutions), have a second person glance at it too. A quick second pair of eyes catches the small things before printing or sending.
  • Keep sensitive files where they belong. Don’t upload confidential sponsor contracts, financial details, or personal team data into places they shouldn’t go, and ask if you’re unsure whether something is okay to share.
  • Be a little skeptical of any surprising claim it makes about facts or people. If it writes a speaker bio or research background, confirm the key details from a real source before you use it publicly.
  • When it offers to delete, overwrite, or move files, read what it’s about to do before you approve. It asks first for a reason, so take the moment to check.
You’re always in control

The one rule that keeps us safe

By default Claude Code proposes every change and shows you a clear before-and-after view with Accept and Reject buttons. Nothing is touched until you click Accept.

  • It works in the folder you open and asks permission before reaching outside it
  • It asks before deleting or overwriting anything
  • Turn off “Help improve Claude” in claude.ai Settings → Privacy so your work is never used to help improve the AI (this is automatic on a Team or Enterprise plan)
  • No real patient data, ever, only conference and sponsor work

Then, how you open it

Three ways in, ranked from easiest to most advanced. Most of the team never needs to scroll past the second one.

Web App, nothing to install

Absolute first try for any doctor, medical student, or organizer. Zero installation, any computer, feel the magic in under a minute. It runs Claude Code right in your web browser, on Anthropic’s own secure computers rather than on yours, so it never touches the files on your machine.

Start here
  1. Open your web browser (the app you use for the internet, like Chrome or Safari).
  2. In the address bar at the top, type claude.ai/code and press Enter.
  3. Sign in with the email and password you just made. (No account yet? Do “Make your account” above first.)
  4. One important switch, and it takes 10 seconds. Go to claude.ai, click your name or initial in the bottom-left corner, choose “Settings”, then “Privacy”. Find the line “Help improve Claude” and tap the switch so it turns gray (off). When it is gray, your work is never used to help improve the AI. That is the only setting you need to touch.
  5. That’s it. You’re inside Claude Code. Nothing to download. Skip ahead to the “first 60 seconds” walkthrough below.
  6. Optional but nice: save the page so you can return in one click. On a computer press Ctrl+D (Windows) or Cmd+D (Mac). On a phone, tap the share or menu icon and choose “Add to Home Screen” or “Bookmark”.

Desktop App, the friendly everyday tool

Your main tool once you’ve tried the web app. A normal program that works directly with your own files, with a warm point-and-click screen and no terminal ever. macOS 13+ or Windows 10/11.

Everyday tool
  1. On a Mac: open your browser, go to claude.ai/code, then click the “Download for macOS” button (or search “Claude Code desktop download” and pick the official claude.ai / claude.com link).
  2. On Windows: from the same page, click “Download for Windows” instead.
  3. When it finishes downloading, you will see it at the bottom of your browser window or in your Downloads folder. Double-click it. On a Mac, a small window opens showing the Claude icon next to your Applications folder. Drag the Claude icon onto the Applications folder (one drag installs it). On Windows, double-click the installer and click through Next, Next, Finish. It’s just clicks.
  4. Open the app. On a Mac from Applications or Launchpad, on Windows from the Start menu. Search “Claude” if you can’t find it.
  5. The first time you open it, a Mac may warn “Claude was downloaded from the Internet, are you sure you want to open it?” Click “Open”. This is normal and safe, and it only asks once.
  6. Sign in with your Claude email and password (same login as always).
  7. Click the Code tab at the top center (yes, it is called Code, that is just its name, not something you do). (The Chat and Cowork tabs are for other things. Code is the one that works with your files.) You’re ready, go to the walkthrough below. Can’t see it? Ask in the team channel and someone will point you to it in one message.

Advanced option: Terminal or command line

You can safely ignore this. Most Med&X members should not use it. The two options above do everything. Included only for completeness.

 Optional
  1. Note for non-technical readers: you do not need this path at all. Use the Web App or Desktop App above and stop here.
  2. The “terminal” is a plain text window where you type commands instead of clicking. Open it. On Mac it’s the Terminal app, on Windows it’s PowerShell.

Your first 60 seconds

The very first thing to try: one small, real request

  • 0–10sThe app (or the claude.ai/code browser tab) is open in front of you. Take a breath. You’ll see a friendly welcome screen. If it asks you to sign in, use your usual Claude email and password. Nothing new to remember.
  • 10–25sNow you point it at your work. If you are on the web app (claude.ai/code), there is no folder to choose. The web version works in its own private space, so to give it a file you click the paperclip or “Upload” button and pick a file from your computer. Or type a request that needs no file at all, like “Make a simple checklist for our next planning meeting.” If you are on the desktop app, click Select folder (choose “Local” first if it asks). A folder is just a place on your computer where files live, like a drawer. Pick one you know, say a Documents folder for a Med&X event. Claude never changes anything without showing you first.
  • 25–40sFind the message box at the bottom. It looks exactly like a chat/text box, the same idea as WhatsApp or email. This is where you simply tell Claude what you want, in plain words (Croatian or English). There’s no special language to learn.
  • 40–50sType one small, real request and press Enter. The best first line needs no files at all: “Write a warm, formal thank-you note in English and Croatian to a speaker who just confirmed for Plexus.” (Once you are comfortable, try one that uses a file, like “Summarize the document in this folder in three bullet points.”)
  • 50–54sIt may reply with a question first, like “What should the note mention?” That is normal, not an error. Just answer in plain words, the way you would text a colleague.
  • 54–60sWatch Claude think and propose the work. It shows you exactly what it is about to do before it does it. For a new document, the content it will create. For a change to an existing file, a clear before-and-after view. Either way you get Accept and Reject buttons, and nothing is saved until you click Accept. When you click Accept, the finished file is saved into the folder you opened (on the web app, a download appears instead). And if it is not quite right, you do not start over. Just say what to change, like “make it more formal” or “add the dinner details”, and it redoes it. That back-and-forth is how you work with it. You just used Claude Code.
